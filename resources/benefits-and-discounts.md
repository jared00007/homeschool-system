# WA Homeschool Benefits, Accounts & Discounts

Reference doc for the non-required but worthwhile stuff — separate from the
legal minimum covered in the README and `resources/links.md`.

## Qualification status

Parent qualifies to teach via **bachelor's degree** (RCW 28A.200.010(4)) —
well above the 45 quarter / 35 semester credit minimum. No parent course or
supervising certificated teacher needed. See `DOI_QUALIFICATION_OPTIONS` in
`tracker/app.py` for the full option list used in the DOI letter.

## The unlock: file the DOI first

Many of the discounts/benefits below want proof you're an "official"
homeschool — usually satisfied by either:
- Your filed Declaration of Intent confirmation (from the district), or
- A membership card from a homeschool org (e.g. HSLDA)

Get one of those before chasing the list below.

## Tax-advantaged accounts (federal — WA has no state income tax break)

- **Coverdell ESA** — up to $2,000/year per child, grows tax-free, covers
  curriculum, tutoring, computers, testing fees
- **529 plan** (WA offers GET or DreamAhead) — up to $10,000/year can be
  withdrawn tax-free for K-12 homeschool expenses under current federal rules

## Free / discounted access

- **Museum passes via library systems** — King County, Pierce County, and
  Spokane library systems lend free museum passes; Pacific Science Center,
  Burke Museum, SAM, and MoPOP all participate. Check your specific library
  system for what's available.
- **Every Kid Outdoors** — free annual National Parks pass for 4th graders
  (check current age/grade window — worth confirming timing against
  Landon's grade)
- **WIAA extracurricular access** (RCW 28A.225.220) — homeschoolers can join
  public school sports/clubs/drama at their resident district — unusual
  nationally, most states don't allow this
- **Homeschool days** — many WA museums/zoos/science centers offer
  discounted admission on designated "homeschool day" dates
- **Running Start** (11th–12th grade) — free community college courses once
  he's old enough
