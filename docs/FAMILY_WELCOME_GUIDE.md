# 🧭 Welcome to Compass!

Hi! You're about to try **Compass** — a homeschool app that builds your kid's
week around what they actually love *and* makes sure they pick up the real-life
skills school skips (money, cooking, adulting). It also quietly keeps your
hours and records for you.

This guide has zero tech-speak. Just follow along. It takes about 15 minutes to
get set up, and then it mostly runs itself.

## 🚀 Getting started (do these once)

### 1. Log in as the parent
Open your **Parent page** → in the left sidebar, click **🔑 Parent access**,
type your **passcode**, and hit **Unlock**.

### 2. Add your student
Sidebar → **➕ Add a student.** Enter:
- Their **name**
- Their **grade** (8th or 9th) — this matters! It sets the whole curriculum.
- The **school year** (e.g. `2026-2027`)

### 3. Set up the kid's website logins
Some learning sites (Khan Academy, CommonLit, etc.) need an account. Go to
**🔑 Accounts** and, for each site, save the **username and password** you
create. A few sites also give a **class code** — there's a spot for that too.
Whatever you save here shows up on your kid's **🔑 My Logins** tab so they can
get in without bugging you.

> **Heads up:** passwords are shown in plain text on the kid's My Logins tab
> (so they can use them). That's fine for their school accounts — just don't
> reuse a password you care about elsewhere.

### 4. Pick a few electives & books
**📚 Curriculum** (parent) → choose a couple of electives and add a book or two
to the reading list. Your kid can also *suggest* ones they want.

### 5. Set the "mix" (the fun part)
Go to **🎛️ Plan Blender** and decide how the week is split between **Passion**
(interest-led projects) and **Foundations** (life skills). A good starting mix
is **60% passion / 40% foundations**. Then:
1. Move the slider.  2. Click **Save mix.**  3. Click **Generate this week.**

Your kid will see the result on their **🧭 My Plan** tab.

## 🎮 How your kid crushes it (the daily rhythm)
Tell them: **open your link, do Today, and end with the Debrief.** That's it.

1. **📅 Today** — their day is a board of cards. Do a subject, tap **"✅ Got
   it."** The card slides to "Done" and a bar fills up. Clear the board and it
   throws confetti. 🎉
2. **🎯 Passion Track & 🧭 Foundations** — when they finish a project or a
   life-skills module, they type **one line about what they did**. That's their
   "proof of work" — it's what makes it count.
3. **⚡ Daily Debrief** (end of day) — two taps: *how are you doing?* and *how'd
   the schoolwork go?* Then tap any "what got in the way?" chips, do the day's
   quick "move" (a stretch or walk), and optionally journal. Takes 20 seconds.

**Your job as parent:** once a day (or whenever), open **🕓 Review & Approve**
and approve the work they logged. *Only approved work counts toward hours* — so
this is how you stay in the loop. Then peek at the **📊 Dashboard** to see how
the week's going and what's been tricky for them.

### Tips to make it stick
- **Same time each day** beats "whenever." The board makes it feel like a game
  with a finish line.
- **Approve daily** so nothing piles up and your kid sees their progress count.
- **Let the passion side be genuinely theirs** — the more honest the interests,
  the better the projects it suggests.
- **The Debrief is only useful if it's honest** — remind them nobody's grading
  it; it just helps you help them.

## 📣 How to give feedback (please do!)
You're helping test this, so your feedback is gold. Two easy ways:

1. **Broken links / typos, from inside the app:** your kid can use the
   **"Report a broken link"** form on their **🔑 My Logins** tab. You'll see it
   in the parent **Accounts** tab.
2. **Everything else — ideas, confusion, "this was clunky," "my kid loved
   this":** jot it down however's easiest and send it to **[Jared]** (text,
   email, a note). Nothing is too small. The stuff that annoys you is exactly
   what makes the app better.

**What's most helpful to hear:**
- Anything your kid *avoided* or found confusing.
- Anything that felt like busywork.
- Moments your kid actually got into it (so we do more of that).
- What you wish it tracked or showed you as the parent.

## ❓ Quick FAQ
- **Is our data private from the other family?** Yes — completely. You have your
  own separate copy; nobody else can see your kid's stuff.
- **Do I have to use the exact schedule?** No. It's a starting point; swap links
  and pick what fits. The life-skills and passion projects are the heart of it.
- **What if my kid marks something done that they didn't really do?** It doesn't
  count until *you* approve it in Review & Approve. You're the check.
- **It's slow the first time!** Give it a minute to wake up; then it's quick.
- **Can they use it on a phone?** Yes — the student link + "Add to Home Screen"
  works great.
