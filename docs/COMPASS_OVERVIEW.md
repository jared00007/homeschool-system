# Compass — What It Is (plain-language overview)

Audience: anyone. No tech background needed. For the developer version see
`COMPASS_TECHNICAL.md`.

---

## The one-sentence version
Compass is a homeschool app that follows what a kid is actually *into* while
still making sure they learn the real-life stuff school skips — and it quietly
keeps all the records a homeschool parent needs.

---

## The problem it solves
Most homeschool tools are either a rigid boxed curriculum (boring, one-size)
or a plain checklist (kids ignore it). And almost none of them teach the
practical things — money, cooking, taxes, basic adulting. Parents also have to
keep hours and records for the state, which is a chore.

Compass tackles all three:
1. **Make learning something a kid will actually run toward**, not away from.
2. **Guarantee the life-skills half** that other tools treat as optional.
3. **Keep the records automatically**, as a byproduct of normal use.

---

## The big idea: two tracks, blended
Compass runs a kid's week as a mix of **two tracks**:

- **🎯 Passion Track** — real projects built around what the kid loves (comics,
  Legos, YouTube, sports, whatever). They tap what they're into, and the app
  suggests projects that fit.
- **🧭 Foundations Track** — the non-negotiable life skills: budgeting, banking,
  cooking, taxes, home repair, digital smarts, civics.

You (the parent) set the mix — say **60% passion, 40% foundations** — and the
app builds the week. The kid gets a plan that feels like *theirs*, and you know
the important stuff is always covered.

---

## How a day flows (the kid's side)
1. Open the **student link** → land on **Today**, shown as a fun **sprint board**
   of cards ("To Do" → "Done").
2. Work a subject block, tap **"✅ Got it"** — the card slides to Done and a
   progress bar fills. Finish everything and it throws confetti.
3. Do a **quest** or a **foundations module** → the kid types one line about
   *what they actually did* (their proof of work).
4. End the day with the **⚡ Daily Debrief** — two taps (how are *you* doing /
   how'd the schoolwork go), a couple of "what got in the way?" chips, and a
   "today's move" (a quick stretch/walk). Optional journal.

Everything a kid marks done becomes a **request**, not an automatic checkmark —
which brings us to the parent side.

---

## How it flows (the parent's side)
1. Open the **parent link**, unlock with your passcode.
2. **Review & Approve** — every block/quest/module the kid finished is waiting
   with their note. You approve (or send back). *Only approved work counts.*
3. **Dashboard** — see hours by subject, this-week-vs-plan, and the debrief
   trends (how they're doing, what's been confusing, roughest subject).
4. **Records/Export** — when you need it, print a clean records packet for the
   state or for ESA reimbursement.

You're the certifier. The kid can log, but nothing becomes "official hours"
until you say so.

---

## What's inside (the tabs)
- **Today / My Week / Calendar** — the schedule as a game board.
- **My Plan** — the blended passion+foundations week.
- **Passion Track** — pick projects that match your interests.
- **Foundations** — the life-skills library.
- **Quizzes / Grades** — auto-graded quizzes, recorded grades.
- **Travel Log** — log national parks / states / cities, a map fills in, journal
  entries with photos.
- **Resources / Accounts / My Logins** — curriculum links, and a safe place for
  site logins + class codes.
- **Grade Scope / Day 1 Checklist** — what a typical grade covers, and a getting-
  started list.

---

## Grades
Compass knows the difference between **8th** and **9th** grade. Set a student's
grade and the daily subjects, links, quests, and the "what this grade covers"
reference all switch to match. 9th grade adds the reminder that high-school work
now counts toward a **transcript**.

---

## Why the records "just happen"
Because every quest and module is tagged to real school subjects, when a kid
finishes one the hours get logged to those subjects automatically. Do the fun
stuff → the compliance paperwork fills itself in. That's the trick.

---

## Who it's for
Homeschool families with a kid roughly grades 5–9, in Washington (the
compliance framing is WA-specific, though the learning model works anywhere).
Right now it runs one private copy per family.

---

## The honest limits (today)
- It's built for **one family per copy** — great for a few families, not yet a
  sign-up-and-go product for hundreds.
- **Photo uploads aren't permanently saved yet** — text records are rock-solid;
  photo proof needs one more upgrade before you rely on it.
- **First load can be slow** (~30–60s) on the free hosting if it's been idle.
